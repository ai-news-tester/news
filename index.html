A groundbreaking artificial intelligence (AI) prediction has forecast the next 20 Ballon d’Or winners, sparking significant debate and fueling speculation. The most surprising predictions involve Barcelona’s Lamine Yamal, who is projected to win three awards, including two while playing for Real Madrid, and the emergence of an unnamed Argentinian player, born in 2020, dubbed “The New Messi.” This AI forecast highlights the unpredictable nature of soccer careers while showcasing AI’s potential for analyzing historical trends and predicting future outcomes. Predicting future Ballon d’Or winners is an inherently challenging task. The dynamic nature of football, with its unpredictable variables (injuries, form fluctuations, managerial changes, and unexpected career shifts), makes accurate long-term forecasting nearly impossible. However, a recent AI-powered prediction, published by Transfermarkt, offers a unique perspective on potential future trends. This projection, encompassing the next 20 Ballon d’Or winners (2025-2044), uses sophisticated data analysis and algorithms to identify patterns and forecast future dominance across various global leagues. The most controversial and intriguing aspects of this projection focus on the future of Barcelona’s Lamine Yamal and the anticipated emergence of a player deemed “The New Messi.” A fusion of established stars and emerging talents The AI’s projections showcase a blend of current stars and promising young talents. Kylian Mbappé (2025), Erling Haaland (2026), and Vinicius Junior (2027) lead the way, reflecting their current dominance and established reputations. This initial phase of the prediction is relatively predictable, but the long-term forecasts become significantly more speculative. Jude Bellingham, Florian Wirtz, and Julián Álvarez are also included.
