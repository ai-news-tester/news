AI-based learning in 5k schools

Bengaluru: The state govt will implement artificial intelligence (AI) and technology-driven teaching methods in 5,000 govt schools to enhance learning.

Bengaluru: The state govt will implement artificial intelligence (AI) and technology-driven teaching methods in 5,000 govt schools to enhance learning outcomes.While responding to a query raised by Dhananjay Darji in the legislative council Thursday, education minister Madhu Bangarappa cited concerns over students' academic performance, especially in mathematics and science. He said the 2023-24 SSLC results revealed a 25% failure rate, with 2,28,763 students unable to clear board exams. He said it pointed to gaps in learning capacity."We discovered that some students could not read or write even after the SSLC examination," Bangarappa said. To address this, he said the education department will train teachers in AI-driven teaching techniques and implement capacity enhancement programmes for science and mathematics instructors teaching classes 6 to 10.The minister said the govt will incorporate suggestions from council members and take further measures to improve learning processes in schools.
